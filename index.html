Topofix — a Blender add-on

From sculpt to finished asset. One panel.

Topofix turns a dense sculpt into a clean, all-quad mesh that keeps its form — then takes it the rest of the way: symmetry, UVs, painting, and a baked normal map, each one click.

Built and battle-tested on real sculpts. No external programs — pure Blender 4.2+.

What it does

One-click quad retopology

Select a sculpt, pick a density, press the button. Form deviation stays well under 1% of the model.

Heals meshes that normally fail

Near-duplicate vertices, hidden geometry, zero-area faces, bad normals — fixed automatically before remeshing, so it just works.

Density goes both ways

Fewer ×½ / Same / More ×2 with a live readout of exactly what will happen. Reduce for games, increase for sculpting bases.

Self-checking output

If a remesh comes back tangled, Topofix detects it and re-runs with safer settings by itself. Results are verified 100% quads.

Perfect Symmetry

Exact mirror in your chosen direction, with a welded, zipper-free centerline seam.

Go to Paint

One click: automatic UVs, a 1K/2K/4K canvas, a paint-ready material, and you're in the Texture Paint workspace.

Bake Detail

The sculpt's fine detail baked onto the low-poly as a tangent normal map, wired into the material, render settings restored.

Batch & non-destructive

Retopologize every selected mesh in one click. The original is always kept as a hidden backup.

Everything lives in one panel

No hunting through menus: density presets with a blue highlight on your choice, a plain-language direction line (▼ Fewer polygons: 195,468 → ~19,547), quality toggles that say what they do, and the finishing tools — symmetry, paint, bake — right below the button that makes the mesh.

Designed so a sculptor who has never retopologized can ship an asset, and a technical artist can still steer every detail.

Honest by design

The panel tells you what will happen before you press anything, the docs state the limitations plainly, and heavy meshes are the only slow ones — typical runs take seconds.

About the creator

Chris Wood is an animator and tool-builder. He taught animation at CalArts, ships his own iOS apps, and builds Blender tools that take the tedium out of a working artist's pipeline — Topofix is the flagship.

Topofix exists because retopology, UVs, and baking are three disciplines standing between every sculptor and a finished asset. They shouldn't be.
